11. 39. Bentley (following a suggestion of Tanaquil Faber, ob.
1672) read Marsi peditis, comparing 11. το. 18 and 111. 5. 9, and
denying et pedites fuisse Mauros et form· et cominus et galeatos in acie
pugnavisse. He supports his opinion, as usual, with great learning,
but Maura· peditis may mean ‘the Moor unhorsed’ and Hor. may have
been thinking of some well-known statue or picture. Cf. Sat. 11. 1. 15.
